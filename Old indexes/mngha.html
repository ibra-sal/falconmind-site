Strategic alignment

Built alongside MNGHA's AI Center of Excellence

MNGHA was designated an AI center of excellence in stroke and cardiovascular diseases in partnership with SDAIA — the Saudi Data and Artificial Intelligence Authority. Falcon Mind is the mental health layer that completes this vision.

  • KAIMRC is the bridge. King Abdullah International Medical Research Center — the largest medical research center in Saudi Arabia — sits inside MNGHA. Falcon Mind's assessment data feeds KAIMRC's research pipeline directly.
  • HIMSS Stage 7 ready. MNGHA is one of the first EMEA systems to reach Stage 7 — full paperless EMR. Falcon Mind is designed for API integration with advanced EMR infrastructure.
  • Saudi Biobank compatibility. KAIMRC's Saudi Biobank and mental health assessment data can be linked — enabling population-level research unique to the Saudi National Guard population.
  • SDAIA alignment. AI scoring and triage built on principles consistent with SDAIA's national AI ethics framework and Vision 2030 digital health objectives.
How it works

From beneficiary to clinical decision in minutes

The platform is designed for the real constraints of a large government health system — minimal clinician time, maximum clinical signal.

1
Beneficiary opens app
Arabic-first. No clinical jargon. Works on any device. Framed as "health check" not "mental health screening" for military populations.
2
Core battery administered
Population-matched core tools. Active soldier: 57 questions, 8 minutes. Family member: 16 questions, 3 minutes. Child: SDQ parent version.
3
AI scores and flags
Scores calculated instantly. AI identifies which conditional tools to add based on score thresholds. Crisis flags surface immediately to the assigned clinician.
4
Clinician reviews
Dashboard shows severity bands, trend over time, triggered conditionals. Clinician makes the final decision — AI recommends, human confirms.
5
Population insights
KAIMRC research dashboard: anonymised population-level data. Track PTSD prevalence across units, burnout trends by hospital, wellbeing across campuses.
